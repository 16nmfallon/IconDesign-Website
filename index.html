What We Do

Custom metal, designed and fabricated in-house.

Every piece is shaped by hand in our Dubuque shop using traditional sheet metal craft and modern tooling. We work in copper, zinc, aluminum, stainless, and galvanized steel.

Metal Roofing

Standing-seam and architectural metal roof systems engineered for energy efficiency and decades of service. Beautiful, durable, and a proven boost to property value.

Copper Cupolas & Steeples

Custom-built copper, zinc, and aluminum cupolas — striking on the skyline and functional for ventilation and humidity control in barns, garages, and homes.

Range & Vent Hoods

One-of-a-kind kitchen hoods designed to your specs. Hammered copper, brushed stainless, blackened steel — the centerpiece your kitchen deserves.

Chimney Caps & Flue Toppers

Handcrafted chimney caps and flue toppers in countless shapes, finishes, and patinas. Protects your masonry while finishing the roofline with style.

Gutters & Downspouts

Seamless and half-round gutter systems in aluminum, copper, and steel. Modern drainage that handles Midwest weather and dramatically cuts repair calls.

Cornices & Trim

Restoration and new fabrication of decorative cornices, fascia, soffit wraps, and historic profiles for residential and commercial buildings.

Custom Doors & Panels

Architectural metal doors, wall panels, and accent pieces fabricated to your drawings — or designed with you from a sketch on a napkin.

Custom Fabrication

If it can be drawn, we can probably build it. Bring us your design and we'll turn it into a piece of finished architectural metal at a fair price.

About Icon Design

Three decades of metal, one careful pair of hands.

Icon Design Architectural Metals was founded by John Herber in 2017 after more than 28 years working in the architectural metal trade with one of Dubuque's most respected sheet metal companies.

Today the shop serves homeowners, builders, architects, and municipalities throughout the Greater Dubuque Area — including Bettendorf, Cedar Rapids, Des Moines, Bellevue, and Madison — with the kind of work you can only get from a craftsman who has spent a career bending metal.

Whether the job is a single copper chimney cap or the metal envelope of a civic building, every project gets the same attention: drawn, measured, and made to last.
